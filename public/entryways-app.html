Accessible doors 4.0

Designing away physical discrimination

Summary

Automatic accessible doors are not entirely automatic or inclusive because they require users to physically actuate a button to open doors, and doors only stay open based on a timer.

I led user research and testing, where we learned that people with disabilities take 13x longer to get through an automated accessible door and are 10x more likely to be injured.

Our team’s north star was to enable people with disabilities to become more independent – to open doors without needing to interact with any physical touchpoint.

A core insight was to design the experience to optimize for that people with limited or no ability to move their limbs – who rely heavily on smartphones for everyday tasks – as the key to making doors accessible to everyone.

I directed user research and testing, design for UX/UI/visuals/interactions, was directly responsible for the mechanical and manufacturing engineering, and managed the product roadmap.

As a result of Portal Entryways App enables its users to open any automatic accessible door hands-free as they approach.
